# Project Plan: Digital Ethics Essay on Piracy

## Perspectives

### Creators (Andreas)

**Arguments for piracy:**
- Wider (equal) access to content in restricted areas (games banned in Australia, other censored content)
- Wider distribution of content

**Arguments against piracy:**
- Creators lose income
- Are not incentivised to create as much content

---

### Consumers (Dang)

**Arguments for piracy:**
- Means for obtaining original efforts (manga, Blood, Taylor Swift catalogue)
- Saves money (until costs become negligible to income)
- Content fragmented across multiple platforms
- Access to censored content and free media (Russia and China)
- Archiving and saving information so that it doesn't get forgotten ([Anna's Archive](https://annas-archive.org))

**Arguments against piracy:**
- Unfair to people who follow the law (paying streaming fees)
- Unregulated and risky: viruses and scams
- Piracy sites need income too
- Promote gambling

---

### General Digital Ownership (Oskari)

- Renting (subscription) vs. owning
- Media platform dependency: the media you purchase is locked into the platform
- If a platform shuts down, what happens to the media you "own"?
- Stop Killing Games initiative: online games can't be played when providers shut down servers
  - [Wikipedia: Stop Killing Games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stop_Killing_Games)

---

### New Perspective

- What aspects of a media piece can you copyright, and what makes it distinct enough to be copyrightable?
  - [Reference video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DW0XUsyBBuY)
- Does the size of the creator affect the morality of pirating? Is it okay to pirate from commercially successful companies vs. indie studios? Where is the limit?
